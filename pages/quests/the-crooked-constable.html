The Crooked Constable

Active S2

A Gaff letter found in the dungeon beneath Droomen Knoll revealed that Constable Sidney Wolder is an agent of the Nag-Lord — the same entity responsible for creating the crookhorns. Wolder has been operating inside Lankshorn's constabulary. The party knows, and Wolder does not know they know.

Objectives

  1. Determine the full extent of the Nag-Lord's network in Lankshorn.
  2. Decide what to do with knowledge of Wolder's treachery.
  3. Determine whether Sgt. Marge Bregson is an ally or another agent.

Leads & Clues

  • The Gaff letter, translated by Llerg, explicitly named Wolder as a Nag-Lord contact. Llerg was bribed to keep silent and has since left Lankshorn for High Hankle.
  • Jack sent a false letter to the constabulary claiming the party would depart on Moot — a day later than planned — hoping to forestall any ambush on the road.
  • Brennan was assigned by Sgt. Bregson to accompany the party as a representative of the town guard. His loyalties remain unclear.
  • Sgt. Marge Bregson's allegiance is uncertain. She may be a genuine ally or part of the same network.
  • Pro-Ramius breggles — Marialla, James, and Fisk — accosted Llerg at the Bogways in Session 3. Session 4 confirmed they are operatives delivering children to Shub's Nana's silver goblins.
  • In Session 4, Wolder was lured into the woods and captured. Under questioning he confessed: he arranged the disappearances to discredit Lord Malbleat, not on direct Nag-Lord orders. He worked through Shub's Nana — a crone who serves the Nag-Lord independently. See the Shub's Nana quest.
