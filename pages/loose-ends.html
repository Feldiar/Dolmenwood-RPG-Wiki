Loose Ends, Rumors, and Miscellaneous Information

Things the party has heard or noticed that don't yet tie to an active quest.

The Abbey S1

Something significant happened at the Abbey approximately 250 years ago, after which it cannot be entered. Shrines in the surrounding woods are connected to this history.

Statue of St. Nuncy the Wretched S1

Shared by Lucien in Session 1: a statue of St. Nuncy the Wretched — a minor saint associated with the downtrodden — once existed somewhere in the region, but was lost centuries ago.

King Pusskin S2

Mentioned by Sgt. Marge Bregson: rumours of a King Pusskin to the south. A shrine to Pusskin stands on the road south toward High Hankle. Milk is the correct offering for those seeking the old cat's favor.

Nightworms S2

Gwomotom knows that nightworms grow large, live in underground tunnels, are edible, but can constrict and eat a person. Immature spawn were spotted on the Ditchway in Session 1.

The Dead Drune Agent S3

A human man in a black cloak bearing Drune markings was found dead in the dungeon beneath Droomen Knoll, drained by stirges. His effects included a golden owl torque, an arcane scroll of unknown content, a platinum ring set with a topaz cut in the shape of an eye, and an orange crystal that triggered a vision in Lucien. Who was this man, and what brought him to Droomen Knoll?

The Whything Stones S3

When Lucien touched the orange crystal taken from the dead Drune agent, he received a vision: a moss-covered stone altar, a slime-coated stag skeleton at its center, standing stones pressing close on every side. A voice spoke: "Ill omens at the Whything Stones. Winter cometh." A second image followed: a tangled forest and metallic owls among the branches. The location and significance of the Whything Stones are unknown.

The Frost Elf Court S3

The dungeon beneath Droomen Knoll is deeply connected to the frost elf court of Prince Mallowheart. The feasting hall contains six murals depicting court members: Prince Seven-Past-Noon (Mallowheart), Sir Dawn-Neverblooms, Lady Silver-So-Mirrored, Lord Slumberbane, Whisper-Of-Candlelight, and Famine-Whistles-Whither. The Drune allied with the Pluritine Church to seal the frost elves' homeland of Frigia using the Ring of Chel. The nature of the remaining court members and the history of the ruins are not yet understood.

The Wood Gods S3

Thinwhistle mentioned wood gods who dwelled in Dolmenwood before the frost elves and were imprisoned by the Drune, who were jealous of their power. No further details are known. Their location and current state are unclear.

The Hlerribuck Breggle Festival S3

The Hlerribuck breggle festival falls on the fourth of Parchment. It has a public portion open to all of Lankshorn and a private gathering for breggles only at Shadhome, the seat of breggle power in the region.

Breggles at the Church S4

Mabble witnessed Father Dobey admit two male breggles to the Church of St. Pastery late at night. Neither returned. Their identities and fate are unknown.

Wolder's Crystal Vial S4

A crystal vial of liquid with a faint nutty aroma was found on Constable Wolder by Jack, who pocketed it. Its contents and purpose are unknown.

The Draining Crystal S5

The Wooden Wolves reported that their companion Brehder touched a large crystal in the wilderness, draining her of all color and vitality — she was left insensible, grey-furred, with milky white eyes. The land around the crystal was also drained and left grey. From a treetop vantage at the end of Session 5, the party spotted what is almost certainly the same crystal: sitting at the center of a grey depression in the earth to the northeast. The nature and origin of the crystal are unknown.

The Golokstone S5

A sandstone slab ten feet tall, cracked down the center, ringed by scarlet toadstools in a forest glade. The markings on the slab are in an unreadable ancient script. The Drune Aetho Grym claims the site as the exclusive domain of the Drune and forbids mortals and fairies from returning. The toadstool spores induce powerful religious hallucinations. The site's purpose and significance are unknown.

The Bishop of Brackenwold S5

Alfred came from Castle Brackenwold — the seat of the Pluritine Church — where he was cursed with lycanthropy. Brother Henrick suspects the Bishop was responsible. Nothing further is known about the Bishop or what is happening at Castle Brackenwold.

The Unicorn in the Wood S5

A unicorn with a rainbow aura, a long horn, and a ten-foot tail was encountered near camp on Parchment 1. It judged a cursed man named Alfred unworthy and refused to lift his curse, but judged Tomkin worthy, temporarily blessing his longsword. A man was pursuing the unicorn, claiming to have found it first. The unicorn's identity, origin, and whether it will be encountered again are unknown.
